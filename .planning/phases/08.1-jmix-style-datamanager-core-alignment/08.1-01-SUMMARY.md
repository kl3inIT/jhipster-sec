## Decisions Made

- Used `@Qualifier("unconstrainedDataManagerImpl")` for the bypass injection because `DataManagerImpl` also implements `UnconstrainedDataManager` once the new seam exists.
- Kept `SecureDataManagerImpl` responsible for row-level spec composition and response shaping, so the new seam does not absorb fetch-plan or merge orchestration.

## Deviations from Plan

### Auto-fixed Issues

**1. [Rule 3 - Blocking] Qualified the bypass bean to avoid `DataManagerImpl` self-injection**
- **Found during:** Task 2 (Add `DataManagerImpl` and rewire the secure facade to delegate through it)
- **Issue:** Once `DataManager` extends `UnconstrainedDataManager`, Spring sees `DataManagerImpl` itself as another `UnconstrainedDataManager` candidate.
- **Fix:** Injected the bypass dependency with `@Qualifier("unconstrainedDataManagerImpl")` so `DataManagerImpl` always delegates to the raw mechanical core instead of itself.
- **Files modified:** `src/main/java/com/vn/core/security/data/DataManagerImpl.java`
- **Verification:** `./gradlew test --tests "com.vn.core.security.data.DataManagerImplTest" --tests "com.vn.core.security.data.SecureDataManagerImplTest"` and the combined focused data-layer suite passed under Java 25.
- **Committed in:** `53653c7`
